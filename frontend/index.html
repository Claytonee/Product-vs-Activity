SOP: Product vs Activity

Impact & Evaluation Tracking Framework

Prepared for Team Leaders | Opportunity Education Tanzania

1. Foundation: Clear Definitions

✅ Product / Indicator

What it is: A measurable, documented output or deliverable that results from completed work.

In simple terms: "What is produced or achieved after the work is done."

✔ Reports, Tools, Datasets, Plans, Completed Logs

❌ Activity

What it is: The action, process, or effort put into doing the work.

In simple terms: "What you are doing, not what you have achieved."

❌ Training, Visits, Meetings, Support Sessions

🧠 2. The 5 Core Criteria for a Valid Product

A documented item qualifies as a Product/Indicator if it meets at least 4 out of 5 of these rules:

1
Tangible
Visible & deliverable
2
Measurable
Can be counted/evaluated
3
Outcome-Based
Shows results, not effort
4
Documented
Stored in files/systems
5
Reviewable
Understandable by others

🔍 3. The Quick Evidence Test

Ask yourself: “Can I attach this as a file or show it as clear evidence?”

🔄 4. The Conversion Formula

Stop writing actions. Start documenting outputs.

📋 Copy [Product Name] + (what was done + what is captured/documented)

Before (Activity): Conduct training for mentors

After (Product): Mentor Training Completion Report (attendance, feedback & competency outcomes documented)

⚠️ 6. Common Mistakes & How to Fix Them

❌ Mistake 1: Using Action Verbs as Outputs

Wrong: Conduct, Train, Support, Hold, Visit

Fix: Convert to nouns: Report, Plan, Dataset, Summary, Log

❌ Mistake 2: Vague or Unmeasurable Statements

Wrong: “Progress made”, “Support given”, “Things improved”

Fix: Attach numbers or deliverables: “30% adoption rate”, “Validated dataset”, “Signed approval document”

❌ Mistake 3: Mixing Products with Weekly Updates

Rule: Products = Final outcomes. Weekly Updates = Activities & progress notes.

Never list an activity under the Product/Indicator column in your tracker.

📊 7. Where Each Belong in Reporting

Section in TrackerWhat Goes Here
Product / Indicator ColumnTangible outputs: Reports, Tools, Datasets, Approved Plans, Completed Logs
Week 1–4 / Weekly UpdatesActivities: Trainings conducted, meetings held, visits made, support provided

🧾 8. Standard Writing Format

All team members must use this exact structure when logging products:

📋 Copy [Name of Product] (key detail of what is captured/measured)

Examples:

  • School Assessment Dataset (data collected and validated)
  • Coaching Report (sessions and outcomes documented)
  • Adoption Summary Report (progress and key insights captured)

🎤 9. Leadership Script:

“Activities show effort, but products show results. We are tracking results. When you log work, always ask: ‘What did we actually produce, document, or measure?’ That is what counts toward our impact.”
